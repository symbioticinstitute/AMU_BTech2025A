📖 AMU B.Tech 2026 Paper Analysis

Exam pattern: 100 questions · 100 marks (changed from 150 marks since 2025). Equal weightage to Physics, Chemistry & Mathematics approximately.

Do you know why cutoff in AMU B.Tech is mostly low?

⏱️ 2 hours for 100 lengthy, calculation-heavy questions → Even brilliant students struggle to finish!

🧪 Chemistry – Moderate to Difficult

As per student feedback and expert evaluation, Chemistry this year was distinct: major emphasis on Inorganic Chemistry with conceptual twists. Most questions were NCERT-based but required deeper understanding, making the section moderate to difficult. Many found numerical parts tricky, yet well-prepared students managed to score.

⚛️ Physics – Easy to Moderate (Formula based)

Physics remained largely similar to the trend of previous year, but comparative analysis reveals it was slightly tougher than 2025. The paper leaned heavily on formula-based problems, direct applications, and conceptual clarity. Overall, students with strong fundamentals rated it easy to moderate.

📐 Mathematics – Most Difficult & Lengthy

Mathematics turned out to be the toughest among the three. Unexpected lengthy calculations, time-consuming problems, and non-routine questions made it challenging. Many candidates reported that the section demanded high speed and accuracy, pushing it to the hardest tier of the paper.

⏳ The Real Reason

If you go through the question paper, you will see they give only 2 hours for 100 questions. The questions involve lengthy calculations and are conceptually demanding. Even a well-prepared, intelligent student finds it difficult to attempt all questions within the given time. This time constraint, combined with complex problems, makes scoring high challenging. Hence, the cutoff remains relatively lower compared to other engineering entrances.
